To: Google Human Resources

The problem of determining what makes a good ad and finding the right user to
see it is a lot like the search for (and discovery of!) the Higgs boson at the
Large Hadron Collider (LHC). In both cases, the analyst has to process large,
multivariate datasets, and make specific statistical statements about targeted
phenomenon in complex systems.

I have spent over seven years doing statistical data analysis at the LHC. My
research consists of creating data analyses to extract meaning from big datasets
and designing novel algorithms to pick the signal from the noise. There are over
4000 members from 42 countries in my current experiment, and I have found great
personal and professional value in collaborating with such a large, diverse
group of people. Because of the highly collaborative nature of science at the
LHC, I ensure my contributions are implemented in a robust, maintainable, and
scalable fashion. One year ago I was nominated by my peers and selected to serve
as the leader of the Tau Physics Object Group, where I coordinate the efforts of
other physicists and determine scientific policy on an experiment-wide scale.

I am ready to translate this experience to a new opportunity with new
challenges, and I believe that a data analysis position with Google is the right
fit. I want to continue to work with a diverse group of smart people, tackling
hard analytical and computational problems using cutting-edge technologies.
There is no better place to do this than Google. I would excited to make my own
unique contribution to the team.

Sincerely,

Evan Friis
